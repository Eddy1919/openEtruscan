Period Feature Explanation
Archaic K before /a/ The K/C/Q tripartition (K before a, C before e/i, Q before u) is diagnostic of 7th–5th century scripts
Archaic No F The letter F (digamma) was reintroduced after c. 400 BCE; its absence suggests an earlier date
Classical C dominant After c. 500 BCE, C replaces K and Q as the general velar stop
Classical Aspirates (θ, φ, χ) Aspirated consonants become more consistent in Classical period orthography
Late F present F (from Latin /f/) appears in late Etruscan as a marker of labiodental fricative
